Frequently asked questions

Common questions about quantum-safe AI compliance.

What is ISO 42001 and why does it matter?

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 is the first international standard for AI management systems. It provides a framework for responsible AI governance, risk management, and compliance. With the EU AI Act enforcement beginning August 2026, ISO 42001 certification is becoming essential for companies deploying AI in regulated markets.

Why is post-quantum cryptography important for compliance?

Current encryption (RSA, ECDSA) will be broken by quantum computers. Under Mosca Theorem, if your compliance evidence needs to remain valid for 10+ years and quantum computers arrive within that window, your evidence is at risk today. SecureGRC uses CRYSTALS-Dilithium (NIST FIPS 204) to future-proof all compliance evidence.

How can you assess compliance without accessing AI models?

SecureGRC uses a metadata-only architecture. We extract model metadata — name, framework, dependencies, lineage, documentation, training provenance — without ever accessing model weights, training data, or production environments. Zero attack surface, zero IP exposure, CISO approval in days.

What is an ML-BOM and why do I need one?

A Machine Learning Bill of Materials (ML-BOM) is a comprehensive inventory of every component in your AI system — similar to how an SBOM catalogues software dependencies. It covers model identity, architecture, training data lineage, framework dependencies, and known vulnerabilities.

How does the EU AI Act affect my business?

The EU AI Act classifies AI systems by risk level. High-risk systems (healthcare, finance, HR, critical infrastructure) must demonstrate compliance by August 2, 2026. Non-compliance penalties reach €35 million or 7% of global annual revenue.
