Frequently asked questions

Understanding quantum-safe AI compliance.

What is quantum-safe AI compliance?

Quantum-safe AI compliance means protecting AI governance evidence with post-quantum cryptography — such as CRYSTALS-Dilithium (NIST FIPS 204) — so that compliance proof remains valid even when quantum computers can break classical encryption like RSA and ECDSA. SecureGRC is the first AI compliance platform to offer this natively.

What is ISO 42001 and why does it matter?

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 is the first international standard for AI management systems. It provides requirements for establishing, implementing, and improving AI governance. With the EU AI Act enforcement beginning August 2026 and fewer than 50 companies worldwide certified, ISO 42001 compliance is becoming a competitive differentiator and a regulatory necessity.

How does SecureGRC assess AI compliance without accessing model data?

SecureGRC uses a metadata-only architecture. We extract model metadata — name, framework, dependencies, lineage, training provenance — without ever accessing model weights, training data, or production environments. This eliminates IP exposure risk and means CISOs can approve deployment without lengthy security reviews.

Why do I need post-quantum cryptography for compliance evidence?

Under Mosca's Theorem, if the shelf life of your compliance evidence plus migration time exceeds the timeline for quantum computers, your evidence is at risk today. Classical signatures (RSA, ECDSA) will be breakable by quantum computers. SecureGRC signs all evidence with CRYSTALS-Dilithium, ensuring it remains verifiable for decades regardless of advances in quantum computing.

When does the EU AI Act enforcement begin?

The EU AI Act enforcement for high-risk AI systems begins August 2, 2026. Companies deploying AI in the EU must demonstrate compliance or face fines up to €35 million or 7% of global revenue. ISO 42001 certification is recognised as demonstrating systematic compliance with the Act's requirements.
